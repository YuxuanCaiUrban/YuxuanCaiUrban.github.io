00 / About
Your Photo Here

Teaching machines
to sense the city

I am a PhD student in City and Regional Planning at UNC Chapel Hill, advised by Prof. Nikhil Kaza, supported by the Doctoral Merit Fellowship. Previously I completed my MA in Computational Social Science at the University of Chicago (Outstanding Thesis Award nominee) and studied architecture at UC Berkeley.

My research sits at the intersection of urban planning, AI interpretability, and public health — probing how vision-language models encode urban perception, building LLM multi-agent simulations for planning decision-making, and diagnosing AI consistency-misalignment issues in spatial reasoning.

01 / Research

Three pillars of
urban AI research

01

Urban Perception & AI

Using crowdsourced data, street-view imagery, and social media to understand how humans experience the city — and training VLMs to replicate and explain that perception at scale.

VLM / CLIPStreet ViewNLPCrowdsourced Data
02

LLM Interpretability & Simulation

Probing internal representations of AI models for urban concepts; developing LLM multi-agent frameworks that simulate planning decision-making and exposing Consistency-Misalignment issues.

Mechanistic Interp.Multi-Agent LLMSHAP
03

Urban Health & Environmental Justice

Examining how long-term environmental exposures — air pollution, heat, green space inequality — shape health outcomes across demographic groups, with ML and spatial analysis.

Environmental HealthGISML / DLEquity
